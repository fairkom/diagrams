## How to give back

This software has been built by people who devoted a lot of time. Free software means that you get the freedom to analyse, adapt and even distribute it (for details check the license).

Certainly you can test the software or use it on a small scale. If you or your organisation deploys it as a service, then we expect you to give back. This can be done in various ways:

-   read and comment issues
-    help with translation
-    fix bugs
-    add features
-    promote it
-    pay the experts

An organisation should consider to allocate an annual budget not only for internal support, but also for hiring experts to help with integration issues or develop add-ons. In addition, we expect organisations to pay an amount to a foundation associated with the software or an IT company of your choice that is actively contributing code and helps maintaining the project. Any amount is appreciated and helps to escape the “tragedy of the commons” when most just take and only a few or nobody gives back.

Proposed one-time amount for production deployment: EUR 500.- minimum (pay [here](
https://shop.fairkom.net/produkt/free-software-fee/))

Proposed annual amount per active user: EUR 10.- (pay [here](
https://shop.fairkom.net/produkt/free-software-fee-annual/))

Feel free to pay a fair amount to any other eligible organisation or person in this open source ecosystem.

Freeriders, who just take the software and give back nothing have to fear bad karma and negative reputation on social or other media channels. If you compare the proposed amounts to the TCO of commercial licenses of proprietary software, free software will usually be still much cheaper!
